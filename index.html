About Me

I am a Mathematics Ph.D. student at UC Santa Cruz, currently studying Modular Representation Theory under the supervision of Robert Boltje. Prior to beginning my Ph.D., I worked as a software engineer at SupplyFrame. I completed my undergraduate degree in Mathematics at Harvey Mudd College in 2017.

Outside of mathematics, I have a wide range of interests and hobbies. These include:
  • Music: I play rock guitar, jazz saxophone, and mix trance and techno music. In addition, I spend way too much time digging for and listening to music.
  • Camping: I backpack and canoe. In the summer of 2013, I spent 6 weeks canoeing down the Kazan river in the Arctic Circle! I spent the two summers after that leading canoeing trips for middle- and high-schoolers.
  • Fitness & Sports: I primarily climb, boulder, snowboard, and do calisthenics. During my undergrad, I powerlifted, and in high school, I played baseball.
  • Games: I play both tabletop games, like Chess and Backgammon, and video games, like Starcraft.
